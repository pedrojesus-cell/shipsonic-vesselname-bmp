# Master Biofouling Management Plan (BMP)
*System Architecture compliant with IMO Resolution MEPC.378(80)*

## 1. Niche Area Management Profiles
The vessel utilizes targeted management protocols for the critical niche zones identified by the IMO to eliminate acoustic shadows and stagnant water fouling.

| Niche Area Location | Primary Risk Profile | Shipsonic Protection Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Sea Chests (Port/Stbd)** | High Risk / Stagnant Flow | 4x Transducers per chest running continuous multi-frequency sweeps. |
| **Bow Thruster Tunnel** | Turbulent Flow / Coating Erosion | 2x Heavy-duty transducers bonded to inner tunnel shell. |
| **Rudder & Pintles** | High Structural Turbulence | 1x Internal wave-guide transducer protecting internal core. |
| **Sea Inlet Piping** | Constant Flow Bio-Layering | Inline transducer rings maintaining clear pipeline internal diameter. |

## 2. Shipsonic Operating Parameters
To prevent macro-fouling settlement, the ultrasonic units must run continuously at the following verified baselines:
* **Acoustic Frequency Range:** Multi-frequency sweep between $24\text{ kHz}$ and $40\text{ kHz}$.
* **Power Input Baseline:** Constant $220\text{V AC} \pm 10\%$, delivering standard acoustic energy output across all structural channels.
* **Target Acoustic Output:** Monitored daily via transducer core impedance tests.

## 3. Contingency Action Plans
If an inspection flags structural fouling or hardware issues, the crew activates these protocols:
1. **System Fault / Power Failure:** If a Shipsonic channel loses power, logs must record the fault immediately. Repairs must be executed within 48 hours using onboard critical spares.
2. **Elevated Fouling Rating (FR > 1):** If a localized underwater inspection reveals macro-fouling (Fouling Rating 2 to 5), a proactive in-water cleaning event via certified ROV must be scheduled at the next port of call.
